Then, later in the 1960's and early 1970's, concentrates containing Factor VIII and IX began to be available. These freeze-dried powdered concentrates could be kept at home and used as needed. They revolutionized hemophilia care. Hemophiliacs were now independent of hospitals. They could travel, hold steady jobs and hope to lead normal lives.
